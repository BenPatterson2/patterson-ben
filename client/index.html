About

I started my journey into software in 2012 when the MIT began offering a version of it's Intro to Computer Science course on edx.org. I caught the bug and studied whenever I had free time. It was difficult path to do it on my own but, I eventually started working in the industry.I love the challenge of a good problem and the satisfaction you get from solving it.

An unusual fact about me is that I'm very interested in testing software. It's odd that a self-taught developer has this focus. I’m drawn to the complexities of a good CI/CD pipeline and it’s promise of delivering higher quality working code.

I put a lot of deliberate effort into keeping up on new technologies and improving my base skillset. If I notice that a lack of understanding or a habit is slowing me down, I'll dig down and work on it until it's no longer an issue.

If I'm not programming, you'll probably find me out for a run, spending time with my daughter or studying something online. Work-life balance is important to me.
